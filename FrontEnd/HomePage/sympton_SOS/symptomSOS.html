Track your body's changes with a trimester-based breakdown of common pregnancy symptoms.

✅ Color-Coded Indicators:

🟢 Normal – Expected symptoms of a healthy pregnancy.

🟠 Monitor – Mild concerns to keep an eye on.

🔴 See a Doctor – Signs that need medical attention.

💡 Expert-Backed Advice: Each symptom comes with practical tips on relief strategies, nutrition, and self-care recommendations, so you can navigate your pregnancy with confidence and ease.

Stay informed, take charge, and enjoy a healthier journey!

TRIMESTER 1

Fatigue

💤 Rest, take naps, and balance rest with light exercise.

Frequent Urination

💧 Stay hydrated but avoid drinking large amounts before bed.

Breast Tenderness

👙 Wear a well-fitting, supportive bra. Use warm or cold packs for relief.

Food Cravings & Dislikes

🍫 Indulge cravings in moderation. Maintain a balanced diet & keep healthy snacks available.

Heartburn

🍽 Eat small, frequent meals and avoid spicy or acidic foods.

Upset Stomach + Vomiting

🤢 Choose bland foods (crackers, bananas) and drink ginger or peppermint tea for relief.

Constipation

🌿 Increase fiber intake, drink water, and use stool softeners if recommended.

Mood Swings

🧘 Try relaxation techniques, engage in light exercise, and seek support if needed.

Serious Emotional Changes

😟 If persistent for 2+ weeks, seek professional help.

TRIMESTER 2

Increased Vaginal Discharge

🩲 Clear, sticky discharge is typical. Wear breathable cotton underwear and change regularly.

Skin Changes (Spider Veins)

🕷 Hormonal changes may cause spider veins. Elevate legs & wear compression socks.

Larger Belly & Breasts

👕 Uterus and breasts grow for pregnancy support. Wear supportive bras & comfortable clothing.

Nosebleed & Stuffiness

🤧 Hormonal changes increase blood flow. Use saline drops or a humidifier.

Skin Changes

🌞 Apply sunscreen for melasma, moisturize stretch marks.

Dental Issues

🦷 Hormonal changes make gums sensitive. Brush & floss gently, use a soft toothbrush.

Back Pain

🦶 Caused by hormonal changes & growing uterus. Wear supportive shoes, maintain good posture.

Braxton Hicks Contractions

🤰 Mild, irregular contractions. Drink water & rest. Contact a doctor if they become regular or painful.

Leg Cramps

🦵 Common at night due to circulation changes. Stretch before bed & stay hydrated.

Dizziness

💫 Due to changes in blood flow. Stay hydrated, rise slowly from sitting/lying positions.

Vaginal Discharge (With Odor or Pain)

⚠️ May indicate infection. Contact a doctor if symptoms occur.

Urinary Tract Infections

🚨 Can lead to kidney infections if untreated. Seek medical attention if symptoms appear.

TRIMESTER 3

Fatigue

😴 Occurs as your body prepares for delivery. Rest, take naps, and avoid overexertion.

Frequent Urination

💧 Pressure from the baby on the bladder. Take breaks & practice Kegels.

Leg Cramps

🦵 Common, especially at night. Stretch, stay hydrated, and stay active.

Vein-Related Discomfort

🩸 Increased blood flow causes swelling. Elevate legs, wear compression stockings.

Shortness of Breath

🌬 Caused by the growing baby pressing on the diaphragm. Maintain good posture, avoid lying flat on your back.

Heartburn & Constipation

🔥 Pregnancy hormones slow digestion. Eat smaller meals, drink water, and consume fiber-rich foods.

Backaches

💺 Hormonal changes and a growing uterus strain the back. Use supportive chairs & apply warm/cold packs.

Swelling of Hands & Feet

💦 Fluid retention is common. Elevate feet, avoid standing for long periods, stay hydrated.

Nausea & Vomiting

🤢 May occur due to baby's position. Stay hydrated, avoid triggers.

Braxton Hicks Contractions

🤰 Can become stronger as labor approaches. Stay hydrated & rest.

Frequent Urination (Leaking)

🚽 Can cause incontinence. Try Kegels, use pads if leaks occur.

Heart Palpitations

❤️ Caused by increased blood volume. Stay calm & monitor symptoms.

Insomnia

🌙 Difficulty sleeping due to discomfort & anxiety. Try relaxation techniques.

Pelvic Pressure

⚖️ As the baby drops lower, increased pressure can occur. Rest & use support belts.

Severe Headaches

⚠ Could signal high blood pressure or preeclampsia. Contact your doctor immediately.

Mental Health & Mood

🧠 Depression & anxiety may increase. Seek professional help if symptoms persist.

Leaking Amniotic Fluid

💧 Could indicate labor onset. Contact your doctor immediately.

Backaches (Severe)

⚠ Chronic pain may require medical help. Consider physical therapy.
